# Sales Frameworks Quick Reference

Use this cheatsheet to select the right framework for the situation. Frameworks are not mutually exclusive; combine them as needed.

---

## Framework Selection Guide

| Situation | Primary Framework | Supporting Framework |
|-----------|------------------|---------------------|
| Enterprise deal qualification | MEDDIC/MEDDPICC | Miller Heiman |
| Complex technical sale | SPIN Selling | Value Selling |
| High-volume SMB qualification | BANT | NEAT Selling |
| Challenger or commodity market | Challenger Sale | Force Management |
| Deep discovery needed | Sandler Pain Funnel | SPIN Selling |
| Multiple buying influences | Miller Heiman | MEDDIC |
| ROI-driven business case | Value Selling | Force Management |
| Initial land deal, plan to expand | Land and Expand | NEAT Selling |
| Buyer-centric modern sale | NEAT Selling | Challenger Sale |
| Deal coaching and review | MEDDIC/MEDDPICC | Sandler Pain Funnel |

---

## MEDDIC / MEDDPICC

**When to use**: Every enterprise deal. Non-negotiable for deals over $100K ACV.

**Core question**: Is this deal real, and can we win it?

| Element | What to Find | Red Flag If Missing |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **M**etrics | Quantified business outcome the buyer expects | No way to build ROI case |
| **E**conomic Buyer | Person with budget authority and veto power | Deal will stall at procurement |
| **D**ecision Criteria | Formal and informal criteria for the decision | You are guessing what matters |
| **D**ecision Process | Steps from "we like this" to signed contract | Timeline is fiction |
| **I**dentify Pain | Business pain driving the initiative, quantified | No urgency, deal slips |
| **C**hampion | Internal advocate with power and personal win | No one selling for you inside |
| **P**aper Process | Legal, procurement, security steps (MEDDPICC) | "Sure thing" dies in procurement |
| **I**mplications of Pain | Cost of doing nothing (MEDDPICC) | Buyer chooses status quo |

**Scoring**: Rate each element 1-5. Composite under 18/30 = high risk. Under 12/30 = likely loss.

## Challenger Sale

**When to use**: Crowded markets where you need to differentiate beyond features. When the buyer thinks they know what they need but is wrong.

**Core question**: Can I teach the buyer something they did not know?

| Phase | What You Do | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Teach** | Lead with an insight that reframes the problem | "73% of engineering teams that track DORA metrics see no improvement because metrics without execution create dashboard fatigue." |
| **Tailor** | Map the insight to the stakeholder's world | "For a VP of Engineering managing 8 teams, that means your coordination overhead will not decrease just because you can see it." |
| **Take Control** | Constructively push back on the buyer's plan | "I know you are looking at dashboard tools, but based on what you have told me, your problem is execution capacity, not visibility. Let me show you what that looks like." |

**Key principle**: Comfortable conversations do not create change. Push the buyer's thinking.

## SPIN Selling

**When to use**: Complex consultative sales where the buyer's pain is not yet fully articulated. Best for discovery calls.

**Core question**: Can I help the buyer articulate their own pain and the value of solving it?

| Question Type | Purpose | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| **S**ituation | Understand current state (use sparingly, research first) | "How many teams are involved in a typical cross-functional initiative?" |
| **P**roblem | Surface challenges they may not have articulated | "What happens when two teams have conflicting priorities on the same initiative?" |
| **I**mplication | Quantify cost of the problem continuing | "When initiatives slip by 2 months, what does that cost in terms of revenue or competitive position?" |
| **N**eed-Payoff | Let them articulate the value of a solution | "If you could cut that coordination overhead in half, what would your team do with that time?" |

**Key principle**: The buyer should talk 70% of the time. Your questions do the selling.

## BANT

**When to use**: Early-stage qualification for high-volume pipelines. Good for SDR qualification before passing to AE. Not sufficient alone for enterprise deals.

**Core question**: Is this worth pursuing right now?

| Element | Key Question | Qualify / Disqualify |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| **B**udget | "Is there allocated budget for this initiative?" | No budget and no plan to create it = disqualify or nurture |
| **A**uthority | "Who signs the contract?" | Cannot identify signer = not qualified |
| **N**eed | "Is this a recognized priority for the organization?" | Nice-to-have, not priority = low probability |
| **T**imeline | "Is there a deadline or event driving this?" | No compelling event = deal will slip indefinitely |

**Limitation**: BANT is necessary but not sufficient. It answers "should we pursue" but not "can we win."

## Force Management: Command of the Message

**When to use**: When you need to align every conversation to measurable business outcomes. Especially useful for training reps on consistent messaging.

**Core question**: Can we articulate our value in the buyer's language?

| Component | Definition | Test |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| **Required Capabilities** | What must the solution do? | Maps to our differentiators, not generic |
| **Positive Business Outcomes** | What improves when they buy? | Stated in buyer's metrics, not our features |
| **Metrics** | How will they measure success? | Tied to their KPIs, with baseline and target |

**Key principle**: If you cannot state the required capabilities, positive business outcomes, and metrics in one sentence, you have not done enough discovery.

## Sandler Pain Funnel

**When to use**: When you need to go deeper on pain discovery. When the buyer gives surface-level answers. When you suspect the real pain is hidden.

**Core question**: What is the full business impact of this problem?

| Step | Example Question |
|------|-----------------|
| Start broad | "Tell me about the challenges you are facing with initiative coordination." |
| Explore duration | "How long has this been going on?" |
| Explore attempts | "What have you tried to fix it?" |
| Quantify impact | "What does this cost you per quarter in lost velocity?" |
| Personal impact | "How does this affect your team's morale and retention?" |
| Quantify inaction | "If nothing changes in the next 12 months, what happens?" |
| Confirm commitment | "If we could solve this, what would that be worth to your organization?" |

**Key principle**: Pain must be felt, not just acknowledged. Drill until the buyer feels the urgency.

## Miller Heiman Strategic Selling

**When to use**: Complex deals with multiple stakeholders. When you need to map all the buying influences and their motivations.

**Core question**: Who are all the people who can say yes or no, and what do they each care about?

| Buying Influence | Role | What They Care About | Your Approach |
|-----------------|------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Economic Buyer** | Releases budget, can veto | ROI, strategic alignment, risk | Executive briefing, business case |
| **User Buyer** | Uses the solution daily | Ease of use, workflow fit, time saved | Demo, POC, hands-on experience |
| **Technical Buyer** | Evaluates technical fit | Integration, security, compliance, scalability | Architecture review, security docs |
| **Coach** | Your internal guide | Wants you to win (for their reasons) | Regular check-ins, arm with materials |

**Key principle**: Every unsold buying influence is a potential no. Map all four and have a strategy for each.

## Value Selling Framework

**When to use**: When building the business case and ROI model. When the buyer asks "why should I spend this money?" When competing on price.

**Core question**: Can we quantify the business value in dollars?

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. Identify value drivers | What business outcomes improve? (Revenue, cost, risk, time) |
| 2. Quantify current state | What is the measurable baseline today? |
| 3. Project future state | What will the metrics be with your solution? |
| 4. Calculate delta | The difference is your value. Express in dollars. |
| 5. Factor in cost | Total cost of solution over the measurement period. |
| 6. Show payback period | When does cumulative value exceed cumulative cost? |

**Key principle**: If the payback period is over 12 months, the deal will be hard to close. Under 6 months is compelling.

## Land and Expand

**When to use**: When the full deal is too large or too risky for the buyer to commit to upfront. When you need to prove value before scaling.

**Core question**: What is the smallest deal that proves our value and creates expansion?

| Phase | Objective | Timeline | Success Metric |
|-------|----------|----------|---------------|
| **Land** | Win initial use case with fastest time-to-value | 0-30 days | Contract signed, deployment started |
| **Demonstrate** | Measurable results that create internal proof | 30-90 days | Quantified outcome the champion can share |
| **Expand** | Use proof to justify broader rollout | 90-180 days | Expansion deal signed, new teams onboarded |

**Key principle**: The land deal must be big enough to be meaningful but small enough to be low-risk. Define expansion criteria upfront.

---

## NEAT Selling

**When to use**: Modern, buyer-centric selling. When the traditional "push" approach does not fit the buyer's culture. Good complement to BANT for deeper qualification.

**Core question**: Is there a real need with economic impact, and can we reach the right people in time?

| Element | Traditional (BANT) | Modern (NEAT) | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|----------------|
| **N**eed | "Do you need this?" | "What is the core business need driving this?" | Depth over surface |
| **E**conomic Impact | "What's the budget?" | "What's the financial impact of solving this?" | Value over budget |
| **A**ccess to Authority | "Are you the decision maker?" | "Can we reach the decision maker?" | Path over title |
| **T**imeline | "When do you want to buy?" | "What event creates urgency?" | Compelling event over arbitrary date |

**Key principle**: NEAT focuses on the buyer's world, not your sales process. Lead with their need, not your qualification checklist.

---

## Combining Frameworks

Most deals benefit from multiple frameworks applied at different stages:

| Deal Stage | Primary Framework | What It Does |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|
| Prospecting / SDR | BANT or NEAT | Quick qualification: is this worth pursuing? |
| Discovery | SPIN + Sandler Pain Funnel | Deep understanding of pain and impact |
| Qualification | MEDDIC/MEDDPICC | Full deal qualification and gap analysis |
| Stakeholder Mapping | Miller Heiman | Identify and engage all buying influences |
| Value Justification | Value Selling + Force Management | Build the business case in buyer's language |
| Competitive Deals | Challenger Sale | Reframe buyer thinking, differentiate beyond features |
| Closing | MEDDIC + Land and Expand | Close gaps, reduce risk with phased approach |
| Expansion | Land and Expand + NEAT | Use proof to justify broader rollout |
