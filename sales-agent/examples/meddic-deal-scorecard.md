## MEDDIC Scores

### Metrics | Score: 4/5

**Evidence**: The prospect has quantified their core problem. VP of Engineering stated in the January 15 discovery call: "We're spending 40% of engineering time on coordination overhead across 8 teams. That's roughly $2.4M/year in engineer time that could be building product." They also cited a 6-month average for cross-team initiatives that should take 3 months.

**What's strong**:
- Pain is quantified in dollars ($2.4M/year) and time (40% coordination overhead)
- Baseline metrics exist (6-month initiative average, 8 teams, current headcount of 180 engineers)
- VP of Engineering has presented these numbers to the CTO in their annual planning

**Gaps**:
- We have not validated these numbers independently. Are they aspirational or measured?
- No agreed-upon success metric for OrgX deployment. What does "good" look like at 90 days?

**Actions**:
- [ ] **Confirm metrics with data**: Ask for their internal engineering time tracking report to validate the 40% claim. *(Owner: AE, Due: 2026-03-25)*
- [ ] **Define success criteria**: Propose a 90-day success framework: 25% reduction in coordination overhead, measured by time-to-complete for a comparable initiative. *(Owner: AE + SE, Due: 2026-03-28)*

---

### Economic Buyer | Score: 2/5

**Evidence**: We believe the CTO (Marcus Webb) is the economic buyer based on the org chart and the deal size ($720K requires C-level approval per our intelligence). However, we have not met Marcus. All interactions have been through the VP of Engineering (Diana Reyes) and her direct reports. Diana has mentioned Marcus is "supportive" but has not facilitated an introduction.

**What's strong**:
- We have identified who the economic buyer likely is (CTO Marcus Webb)
- Diana reports that Marcus is aware of the initiative and "supportive"

**Gaps**:
- **Critical**: We have zero direct contact with the economic buyer. "Supportive" from a champion is not the same as direct engagement.
- We do not know Marcus's decision criteria, priorities, or concerns
- We do not know if there is a board-level approval required at this ACV
- We have no read on Marcus's relationship with Competitor Y (see Competitive Situation)

**Actions**:
- [ ] **Get the meeting with Marcus**: Ask Diana directly: "For a deal of this size, we'd typically expect to meet the CTO to understand their strategic priorities. Can we set up a 30-minute call?" If she resists, probe why. *(Owner: AE, Due: 2026-03-22)*
- [ ] **Prepare executive briefing**: Build a CTO-specific briefing focused on strategic engineering capacity and competitive velocity, not product features. Marcus cares about "can we ship faster than competitors," not DORA dashboards. *(Owner: AE + SE, Due: 2026-03-25)*
- [ ] **Map approval chain**: Ask Diana: "Walk me through what happens after your team recommends us. Who else needs to sign off, and what do they care about?" *(Owner: AE, Due: 2026-03-22)*

### Decision Criteria | Score: 3/5

**Evidence**: Diana shared an internal evaluation rubric during the February 5 call. Criteria include: (1) Time-to-value under 30 days, (2) Integration with existing GitHub + Linear stack, (3) Security compliance (SOC2, SSO), (4) Demonstrated ROI within 90 days, (5) "Strategic alignment with our AI-first engineering vision."

**What's strong**:
- We have the formal evaluation criteria in writing
- Criteria 1-4 are concrete and we can address all of them with evidence
- Our POC plan directly maps to criteria 1 and 4

**Gaps**:
- Criterion 5 ("strategic alignment with AI-first vision") is vague. We need to understand what this means specifically to Marcus and the leadership team. This could be our strongest differentiator or a trap if they have a different definition of "AI-first."
- We do not know if there are informal criteria not on the rubric (e.g., relationship, risk aversion, vendor size)

**Actions**:
- [ ] **Clarify criterion 5**: Ask Diana: "When Marcus says 'AI-first engineering vision,' what does that look like in practice? What has the team already tried?" *(Owner: AE, Due: 2026-03-24)*
- [ ] **Map our evidence to each criterion**: Build a decision-criteria alignment doc showing how OrgX addresses each of the 5 criteria with specific proof points. *(Owner: SE, Due: 2026-03-28)*

### Decision Process | Score: 2/5

**Evidence**: Diana described the process as: "My team evaluates, I recommend to Marcus, and then procurement handles the contract." This is likely an oversimplification of an enterprise $720K deal. We have not confirmed the full process.

**What's strong**:
- We know the evaluation team and have good engagement with them
- Diana is willing to describe the process (not hiding it)

**Gaps**:
- **Critical**: No mention of security review, which is standard at this ACV and company size. We know Velocity Financial is SOC2 certified, so they almost certainly have a vendor security review process.
- No mention of legal review for a $720K contract
- No mention of procurement timeline. Enterprise procurement at financial companies often takes 4-8 weeks.
- 6-week close target (June 30) is extremely aggressive if security + procurement have not started
- We do not know if there is a board or finance committee approval step

**Actions**:
- [ ] **Map the real process**: Ask Diana: "For a contract of this size, can you walk me through every step from 'we want to buy' to 'contract signed'? We want to make sure we are not surprised by timeline." *(Owner: AE, Due: 2026-03-22)*
- [ ] **Start security review proactively**: Send our SOC2 report, security questionnaire, and architecture docs to Diana now, before she asks. This saves 2-3 weeks. *(Owner: SE, Due: 2026-03-22)*
- [ ] **Adjust close date expectation**: Internally, model close date as August 31 (add 8 weeks for security + procurement). Brief leadership on realistic timeline. *(Owner: AE, Due: 2026-03-25)*

## Coaching Notes

Questions the manager should ask the AE in the next deal review:

1. **"Have you personally spoken to anyone at Velocity other than Diana and her direct reports?"** (Tests multi-threading. If no, this is a single-threaded enterprise deal, which is a red flag.)

2. **"What happens to this deal if Diana leaves Velocity tomorrow?"** (Tests champion dependency. If the answer is "it dies," the deal is not qualified.)

3. **"When Diana says Marcus is 'supportive,' what specific evidence supports that?"** (Tests for happy ears. "Supportive" without evidence is dangerous.)

4. **"Walk me through the exact steps from 'Diana recommends us' to 'contract signed.' How many weeks does each step take?"** (Forces the AE to confront the timeline reality.)

5. **"If you had to bet your commission on this deal closing June 30, would you?"** (Gut check. If the AE hesitates, the forecast is wrong.)
